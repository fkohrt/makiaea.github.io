an everlasting vision / of an ever–changing view

soon within my tapestry / along the rutted road

maintaining relationships

a lot of people will only be your friend if you appear to share all their opinions with them. this is the myth of identity. it goes much deeper, as people in positions of authority and power, like supposed teachers and mentors and police and doctors, will feel threatened if you do not have the same worldview as they do, because their self–worth and identity and happiness are chained to their values — and no matter how wonderful a person you are, they will treat you as a threat to themselves, their supposed society, their supposed civilisation. they will abuse you, suppress you and kill you, all because their values say you are a threat. what can we do about such “values”? the short answer is “not much” — there is no way to force a person to enlightenment. the better answer is to find and make friends with people who have broken the shackles of their values, and to create and grow a society and a civilisation that cherishes everyone

  • for some, the veneer of civilisation is thin, brittle, easily broken, torn. but we cannot create a civilisation while hiding from all abusers and oppressors — because we would also be hiding from each other

expectations set by interactions with others - like the seeding done by exposure to random number influences numerical estimates, so also do the conditions under which we live and work alters our expectations and thinking.

in relationships, failure to understand what it takes to maintain and grow a relationship leads to the failure of the relationship, not a mysterious “falling out of love”. if you don't achieve together, you fall apart. and when your partner or friend does not or cannot share your dreams then it is much harder to achieve something together.

don't assume that one thing leads to another you may have to leave those friends behind that only want to be friends because they think you are similar to each other — as you grow you will probably grow differently before you grow similarly.

like children born in a war, or someone whose parents are separated and always fighting, how can we learn what a real relationship is like? the only way is to find and observe others around us who are making real relationships and if that is not available, to find clues in stories and imagine what it is to make a real relationship work. that is why i love stories so much, and why imagination is so important.

false economy to be miserly with friendship, must be generous to share friendship even if only one in ten people is open enough to become best friend. am willing to take the risk of rejection because we may share a beautiful friendship together.

language of heart–to–hearts is not written or spoken language,

trusting each other means that communications are much easier, as meanings of interactions are clearer, for example trust that what is said is literally true or the best possible interpretation of this.

in communication, as well as translating and other informational arts involving words, and more generally, we would be deluding ourselves if we believed there is an exact translation or match from one word–meaning to another. rather, there is a region, a cloud of possible meaning, dependent on context, within which the intention resides.

  • to learn an language or to communicate fully with another person we need to understand first the gist of the meaning (thus the exact translation can sometimes be misleading compared to a rough translation) and then must proceed from there to understand the particular meaning given by the speaker or writer or originator.
