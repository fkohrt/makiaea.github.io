origin of and how to stop abuse

what kind of society makes monsters that turn orphaned, powerless, vulnerable children into abused slaves, sexual objects, living weapons?

  • the society we build every day with our own two hands — if we let the powerful rape and pillage the powerless — if we let society make abusers powerful
  • it is abuse to “sacrifice the few to save the many” — the answer is in not making a society that asks us to do this, where the rich and powerful take so much that the poor and powerless must fight each other just to live
  • each and every one of us is part of the problem — and part of the solution

once, a long time ago, they too loved, but they lost it.

is it our response to that inevitable loss that determines our individual fates?

  • what the abusers can never have is true love and friendship
  • power as destroyer. people who forget how to create can only destroy. their lives are empty of real love and connection, not because no one would love them, but because they can no longer accept love.
  • abusers are those who were, possibly through no fault of their own, unable to deal with the abuse they faced as children
  • people are so disappointed by the abuse they face in the world, that the become hardened and perpetuate the poison that destroyed them

we unintentionally act harmfully, and this will always be true. but we can learn to do better, and try to repair the harm we cause

  • all people are abusers, some deny it is so
  • what is it like to understand that without constant vigilance and massive effort on your part, and a whole lot of luck which you may not get, that you will end up being as abusive as the previous generation, and all those that came before them?
  • people often believe that what they are doing is responsible, even if in fact it is abusive. so sometimes it takes an extraordinary turn of events before people are willing and ready to re–examine how we live our lives.
  • we all have moral failings. those that think they do not are delusional. but that does not mean we should not work at resolving our failings, making them into successes.

because people trap themselves in their circumstances, they feel they cannot escape and feel as if they have no options. to make themselves feel better, some may use others, perhaps unkindly.

  • their lives have become empty, and they try anything to fill the void within, but they fail, for the void can only be filled by one thing — themselves
  • people so trapped by their own pain they care not the pain they cause others, nor can help each other recover.

abusive people are in pain but this does not absolve them of responsibility.

  • abusers may be wrong, but this doesn't stop them from having a horrible impact on the earth and our lives.
  • abusers aren’t wrong — if you want to live with abusers.
  • does not mean abusers abusing okay, nor that they are worthless

the concept of selfishness can be misleading, because it implies that people intend to be selfish — but perhaps most people who act selfishly, who act abusively, do not, for whatever reason, currently have the ability to think how their actions affect others.

  • It may be, that in the human evolutionary past, that selfishness served a useful purpose — rather than being abusive. Our response to threats and scarcity may have been adaptive, on a small scale in dire emergencies and even then the effect was limited by the small reach of humans. But to speculate on this may not be worthwhile, as there might not be an answer to find. What we need to deal with is the huge abusive effect that our human selfishness causes now in the world, because our cumulative impact is now so massive.

people do not intend to become abusive. people slowly build up bad habits and attitudes that lead to worse things. the same is true of good things however.

  • It is not that people are evil; even well–meaning people do harm when they don't try to understand what they do. It isn't their prejudice or your difference from them, it is a lack of care that is innate because they are not ready.

with beauty and power too, the privileged are more often irresponsible in their behaviour. it is often interesting to see how people behave towards you when they believe you are not as privileged as they are.

  • we need to understand that there will always be people like this. but that does not mean we should let them take power over us.

people have a justification system for their beliefs and behaviours. one that allows them to say that the victims that they make deserve their fates.

  • what causes this? is it because of an imposed or self–imposed scarcity, a paucity of options?

abuse, is, at heart, the inability to reconcile our inner representation of reality — with reality as it truly is.

  • because reality changes, we must change our inner representations of it to match. the problem comes when we no longer do so. this is why change, and momentum — the flow of change — are such essential ideas and skills.

The baseball cards create a “pointillist picture” of staff members, aggregating across review cycles and dots, and incorporate various assessments that employees take. The cards’ display changes over time, revealing who’s best suited to play each position, and flagging areas to “rely on” and “watch out for” with green and red lights.

    When you express an opinion, it’s weighted by whether you’ve established yourself as believable on that dimension. In presenting your views, you’re expected to consider your own believability by telling your audience how confident you are. If they think the behavior is safe, we should emphasize all the good things that will happen if they do it—they’ll want to act immediately to obtain those certain gains.
