reality vs illusion

causation defines our speech because it is used to define words — it is circular. we define things by what they do but what they do is dependent on causality

Illusion as what is left when description does not match reality, when a map does not match the territory. So anything can become illusory when the reality it means to describe undergoes change or otherwise no longer matches the description. Accuracy of information is the degree to which it matches reality or the degree to which it minimises illusion.

  • Good and Evil are illusions. You will probably already be able to understand why this is.

we are blessed and cursed at the same time. by being able to see through some of the illusions that flow round us. through false illusions and false comfort. to love dearly with all our hearts yet to be forever apart.

  • the reason we seem to be different from the norm is that we do not believe in the sanctity of what they tell us. so we are often closer to reality than their static descriptions of things–as–they–may–have–been.

reality is different to what most of society tells us. not so different that we can never reach it —yet what they tell us is sufficiently misleading that as long as we believe it, we will not.

purity, perfection are myths, and if you want to go that far, so too are independence, fate, belief, necessity, power, values, words, and myth itself. that doesn't mean they are any less important, in some ways we should be striving for more of each myth, not less.

  • memories and habits are spells, as are words and songs and stories and science and myth.
  • so–called perfection is satisficing at a high level

we may believe our lives are full of logic or reason, but viscerally we know this is not as we believe.

illusion of mastery, illusion of certainty, illusion of control

illusion of separateness

people operate from their certainties, that just seems to be the way it is. but if you know otherwise, you will not be able to convince them. what then can you do?

just because some things we observe seem incorrect, does not imply that all things we think are incorrect are actually incorrect.

misusing kuhn’s paradigm model, we could say that paradigms must necessarily exist by definition because for a paradigm to exist then most people (at some point) must not understand the issue, and so there is a paradigm to overturn, ad infinitum.

assuming that just because something is common and negative, that we should also feel negative. example: abusive behaviour. this is because we assume what we know is true, and that it will continue to be true — the denial of change.

instead of seeing what is there, we mostly see what we want to see, what we expect to see, what the story leads us to believe we should see.

things are rarely what we think they are

  • but that is okay unless we insist that what we think is really how things are

myth: misunderstanding will (to choose), value (of chosen actions), and ability (to do chosen action).

hubris from thinking we know something of reality, when actually we just borrow a little temporary knowledge. origin of this is completion fallacy.

by their very nature, the most difficult problems are those at which we are most likely to fail. not the most severe, or even the most common, but those where there are more opportunities for us to make mistakes.

so there wasn't a time when we knew that things were true and when things were not.

most people think they don't form an attachment to a place or person until they have shared many moments and memories together. but like many things we believe, this isn't often true.

generalisations are illusory knowledge, like much of our so–called knowledge it seems to work most of the time — at present. it may be that most of our knowledge is this illusory kind — we would do well to understand this

the fact of the matter is, the truth hurts. not because it is inherently painful, but because we are living in denial of reality and to face reality suddenly is shocking.

the issue is not that people are wrong — we are all wrong much of the time and unaware of it… the issue is given this fact, what do we do? one answer is to often review our assumptions, to try to make assumptions that are less wrong.

mistaking pride or confidence, height or a low voice — physical attributes — for competence or ability

life on earth is just a fragile thin layer, like neocortex. may seem permanent like civilisation but actually ephemeral.

illusion: we can only expect of people what they can give, and no more.

  • if this is true, how would we ever improve?

i don't believe in the "10000 hours to mastery" meme because it is a myth. yes there are people who have spent ten thousand hours towards some things but that does not mean that all people need to spend ten thousand hours in the same way, or that they must do so. that is another old spell, the spell of specialisation, dedication and concentration.

life not zero sum

  • don't have to lose so someone else can win
  • the world of not zero sum. just because someone cannot see your worth does not mean you are worthless. just because someone else got the opportunity you wanted does not mean that you have lost. the time you used may be gone but you have gained and learned from the experience and possibly also from the relationship you have created.

being acutely aware and at the same time being easygoing may seem impossible, but it is actually possible, and is actually much easier than is generally believed

Dichotomies are bad because restricting dialogue to two theories, rather than accepting full reality, is not a simplification, it is a falsehood.

  • We should revisit binary decisions. They may not be truly binary anymore, and may never have been so.

the reason we are so resilient, and why abusers are so fragile, is the same — our relation to reality. an abuser’s life is far from reality, so when their illusions are unmasked there is reckoning to pay. we are still far from reality, just not as far as an abuser is, so shocks have less effect on us as there is less perceptual distance to fall

we must experience the search for truth before we can appreciate that there is no real truth that can be found — otherwise we are unlikely to believe such a thing. “the way that can be described is not the way”

perversion of darwin’s theory of evolution, illusion of competence and competition

entropy

  • I dispute that entropy is fundamental to the universe. I just don't like it, just as I don't like schrödinger's cat.
  • the worship of order over seeming disorder is mistaken. odum knew as much when he criticised people for assuming nature to be simple rather than a complex ordered system. in almost the reverse of this, I believe we may be mistaken that complexity is a desired outcome, even though odum admits simplicity has its place. I believe they are our categories which are not really relevant to reality outside of our conception of it.
  • it could be that humans are supposed to have this explosive growth (if you believe in probability) so that at least a few of us are well adapted in the darwinian sense to the environment of the earth after humankind crashes to sustainable levels again.
  • it is an error to believe that all explosive growth is bad, perhaps sometimes it is necessary in order to overcome some obstacle that could best be overcome in this way.
  • odum assumes overpopulation is necessary for selection, as is the death of some of that overpopulation. I think it suffices that some do not reproduce, but the assumption that it is the best that reproduce is erroneous and self–serving.
  • it is an illusion of human desire for control and order that the desired theory to represent reality should be based on those principles.
  • most people believe that they need these principles to be true for them (the people) to survive. this is untrue. they are addicted to those principles and this type of addiction leads them to believe in this way. this could be the main reason why it can be difficult for people to get to grips with what we say — if this challenges this addictive belief, but they cannot overcome their addiction, they will not be able to change their belief. so again it comes down to what kind of person can already overcome their addiction and what people can do to learn how to do this. against this are arrayed abusive people who find it suits their way of life for most people to be addicted to their situation.
  • and to go on to why those people are abusive is the topic of the next section. i.e.:
  • people act abusively when they are in the habit of doing so. which means all sorts of things like how important our choices are because how we choose makes a great difference to whether and how we maintain habits. this is why so–called "freedom" is important. and closeness to current reality is not a common feature of habit.
  • so the process of becoming responsible is often at first similar to stopping an addictive habit like smoking.

I don’t mean to be harsh on the authors of the paper — it is just an ordinary example of what we are all accepting as truth, when reality is still hidden from our awareness

    • “Physical cleansing may wash away moral sins through symbolic self-completion (10); that is, people are motivated to complete their self-definitions (e.g., musicians) when indicators or symbols of this definition are lacking (e.g., skills) by engaging in activities that complete the symbols (e.g., training). Thus, when moral self-definition is at stake, such as when one has indulged in morally questionable activities, one should naturally be motivated to engage in activities that will restore moral integrity. For instance, Tetlock and colleagues (11) have shown that the mere contemplation of violating one’s core values spurs intent to take actions that will restore and protect those values. The restoration or completion of the moral self can be achieved through direct restitution, but it may also be achieved through substitutable symbols or activities that are not directly related (10, 11). Given the demonstrated association between physical cleansing and moral purity, cleansing activities that improve physical cleanliness may also compensate for moral impurity. Thus, we expected that a threat to the moral self would motivate the restoration of moral purity through direct compensatory behaviors (e.g., volunteering to help). If, however, physical cleansing restores the moral self, then individuals should have less need to engage in direct compensatory behaviors after physically cleansing themselves. This is indeed what we found. In Study 4, participants described an unethical deed from their past (the same recall task as in Study 1). Afterwards, they either cleansed their hands with an antiseptic wipe or not. Then they completed a survey regarding their current emotional state (9). probability of precipitation

  • POP is the probability of exceedance that more than a certain amount of precipitation, e.g. 0.01 inches(USA NWS) or 0.1mm(UK MetOffice), will fall in a single spot, averaged over the forecast area. This can be expressed mathematically:

    PoP = C x A

    C = the confidence that precipitation will occur somewhere in the forecast area.
    A = the percent of the area that will receive measurable precipitation, if it occurs at all.
    For instance, if there is a 100% probability of rain covering one half of a city, and a 0% probability of rain on the other half of the city, the POP for the city would be 50%. A 50% chance of a rainstorm covering the entire city would also lead to a POP of 50%. The POP thus usually expresses a combination of degree of confidence and geographic coverage.

    Note that the POP measure is meaningless unless it is associated with a period of time. NWS forecasts commonly use POP defined over 12-hour periods (POP12), though 6-hour periods (POP6) and other measures are also published. A "daytime" POP12 means from 6 am to 6 pm.2
