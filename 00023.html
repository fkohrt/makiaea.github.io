faith and doubt

i think about what we believe and how it dictates our reality.

  • what we think of the world is a better indicator of what we believe ourselves rather than what is actually in the world.
  • as long as people believe in “you are for is or against us”, they believe in a distorted reality, which unfortunately is never true, not even momentarily.

chunking — belief systems may be our way of chunking reality, like advanced chess players think of similar or even fewer numbers of moves than beginners, only the moves themselves are more advanced due to chunking (like words instead of letters) — belief systems are patterns we use to make sense of reality, and may be all we have even though they are inadequate.

faith and doubt belong together, allows renewal, hope. faiths without doubts are tyrannies. doubts without faith is despair.

illusion of being right, illusion that there is a right answer — leads to inability to learn

  • what is odd about people who worship statistics is that they intellectually know they could be wrong, but because the probability of that seems low to them, their belief allows them to stop questioning their faith.

we can have everything we want except the belief we can have it.

anything that requires you to pay obesiance is a devil, not a god. why would god want your obedience? is it any different if you obey voluntarily? perhaps God as father makes some sense here, if we were wayward children.

I still love travel even though I no longer travel much at all — what I do now is not as representative of my beliefs as people would believe it is for everyone.

  • but this is not because I am unusual, rather it is because the usual belief about what people's behaviour shows about themselves is not true. This is an example of how our beliefs can constrain our world without us understanding that they do.
  • why do I not travel more to see the wonders of nature? just because I can does not mean I must. If everyone were to do so, there would be no nature left. When I do not need to, I do not do so.

perhaps we can no longer be coerced by the threat of the unknowable when the threat of the knowable has already become just as great. could this be why religions are declining?

doubt is a positive thing, unlike what they say. when you can imagine even a small event happening differently to how you were told, your world becomes richer, not poorer. especially when people are trying to manipulate you, this is one of the few practical ways to defend against their manipulation. because when even something very small is not what they say it is, your understanding and interpretation and appreciation of something or an event or an action can be completely different to that it would be without that little reinterpretation.

god and baby buggies. like a forward facing buggy, we sit as a young infant child, seeing what is in front of us. but if we don't turn around or listen, do we ever know there is a parent who pushes our pram, until we can walk on our own?

a lot of people define themselves by their beliefs, in effect letting their beliefs define them.

belief can be an anchor, a rock. but unfortunately it can also be a fetter, a burden.

it is as if they have a spell cast on them, from which it is hard to break free

i understand god the parent, god the friend, god the presence — “father, son, spirit” but i have no truck with people who take god the saviour as literal truth. i believe nobody can save us, but that they can help us save ourselves. i believe salvation and paradise should be taken metaphorically, not literally.

belief in own worth

we are often exhorted to be confident our courageous, but these qualities come from belief in our own abilities and our capacity to deal with things. that is why these traits are good or attractive, because they show our capabilities. it also follows that if we wish to be truly confident or courageous then what we need to do is learn, reflect on, and build our capabilities.

belief in worth of others

this is often called idealism by people who do not understand what it means that everyone is someone worth cherishing.

interpreting attachment theory

  • lacks belief in own worth
    anxious attachment style

  • lacks belief in worth of others
    avoidant attachment style

  • lacks belief in own worth and worth of others
    anxious-avoidant attachment style

  • has belief in own worth and worth of others
    secure attachment style
