reading

you are not so smart

david mcraney 2011
9781101545355

  • the misconception: you are a rational, logical being who sees the world as it really is.
    the truth: you are as deluded as the rest of us, but that’s ok, it keeps you sane.

  • priming

    • the misconception: you know when you are being influenced and how it is affecting your behavior.
      the truth: you are unaware of the constant nudging you receive from ideas formed in your unconscious mind.

By not normally taking notes of what I read and later accepting those notes as the distillation of the material, I remain open to the fact that there is much in any material that I may not understand. By not distilling the material myself, instead rereading when necessary, I let the original form remain to inspire as it was intended to inspire, and it may be that on subsequent readings I have a different interpretation of the messages contained in the material, because I have a different understanding and approach to life (or my own life).
