effective methods

Thoreau: “simplify the problem of life, distinguish the necessary and the real.”

  • less but better: doing only what is essential. distinguish vital few activities, eliminate nonessentials and obstacles so essentials have smoother passage. understand our best contribution and make doing it easier.

General principle of getting only what you can fully make use of; combine with general principle of preparing for the situations you can anticipate.

can sing loud without blowing out a candle in front held in front of the mouth

  • in other words, the volume of sound comes not from the volume of air expelled but from shaping our bodies to resonate with the sound

pronounce words using tongue and teeth and upper lip without using lower lip, vowels from shape of back of tongue rather than shape of front of mouth

  • keep mouth more open for resonant sound when singing

can swim without needing to kick

  • the arms act as “anchors” in the bottom of the stroke; we are gradually pushing against these anchors as we gain speed, while elongating the rest of the body (by holding other arm in front and keeping legs extended) to help movement of body smooth through water
  • how to describe swimming speed
    swimming speed is “proportional to the the length of the body in water”. but is this is really the case? actually how it should be said is “all other things being equal, etc.” the length of the body in water is an explanation for speed, not the cause of it. the cause is reducing resistance.

listening to music at low volume

we can build with imagination what the piece is meant to sound like (especially if we have heard the piece before), whereas with high volume you have no choice in the matter, it is thrust upon you.

third try to master, average time six months since learning so many different things at any one time

  • can adapt instantly to events, ten days continuous is where mastery begins. new language, martial technique, computer. e.g. computer comes to approx six months on and off usage.

I choose not to learn particular ideas well, deliberately. because I believe my creativity in creating and merging ideas comes from being less shackled to particular ideas than others have been. unfortunately almost everyone else believes in specialisation so will actively denigrate what I do. in a way it is like a self–induced dyslexia — blurring the individual truths to see better the greater truths.

The test where to check the pattern you need to find the card that refutes the theory, not confirms it. popular conception of scientific method is wrong, not searching for confirmation — rather, for verification. we need to look for that single disproof, when it is a true disproof, we can move on to another issue. that is how we can be truly effective in our learning and doing, though almost nobody yet understands this.

  • there may be no way to prove a theory, for theories are not truths. they are guesses, and one of the most effective ways to move forward from a mistaken theory is to find evidence which refutes it. this is what works if you are not emotionally dependent on the theory you are testing, this does not work well if you are reliant on that theory to provide something otherwise missing from your life
    • change conditions of game test to ones where we are detecting abuse — are the test’s aims and our ability to solve them clearer?

mixing and composites

  • idea of mixing is to use strengths of things or people where they are most appropriate
    • if you deny mixing, you deny a fundamental property of the universe

  • what is the central dogma of a person our a group our a society? there too will be the most fallow ground for understanding what they are not understanding
    • judo for the mind
      • understanding where you think your stability is, or understanding where it really is, is useful when you want to move it, or change it.

      • ability to control experiment
    • lies
      • why are lies so bad? don't get me wrong, I hate lies probably more than most people, but what exactly do I hate about lies? is it really because of trust? or is it something more subtle than my present understanding?

scheduling and adding timed alarm for recurring tasks lets you “decide in advance” what you will be doing, removing the procrastination of infinite choice when you could decide to do anything.

logistics, tactics, as hidden way of winning. be it wars, by building and destroying supply lines, or commerce, by creating better processes, or friendships, by ensuring meetings and communications.

anger at abuse can be used effectively to bypass internal obstacles. it is a useful motivator to action.

  • the body is not suited to abuse and over–specialisation. it is much easier for it to repair itself when it is not exposed overmuch to extremes. this is not to say exposure to some extremes such as anger and excitement are unnecessary. but in most situations it is good to be moderate.

confidence of action is important, to have an option ready to go in case you meet a situation that needs an immediate response

if we don’t make the effort to improve and plan for emergencies, we will only get worse — for during an emergency you have virtually no time to improve, only to use what you already have, so therefore you do not improve, you get worse

a (flexible) schedule can ease the way between tasks and help us return to continue work on a task. flexibility is important for dealing with unexpected events

“forced efficiency” due to less resources may be less about focus than about not spending time to factor in the different ways of doing things that would be advantageous by using those (now unavailable) additional resources

what may seem easier may be in fact less effective.

  • picking the so-called low-hanging fruit first is the wrong way to go about it. it may make sense in an industrialised world but that world has come to an end. pick the ripe ones at the top first, then at the middle, then lowest down. in accordance with gravity and the laws of nature this is how it should be.
