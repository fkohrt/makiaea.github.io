emotion and logic, science and religion, thinking and doing — some people revere them. they are not the only ways to describe or live in the world

it might seem paradoxical or ironic, that the people least suited to wielding power responsibly are those that seek it. that those who seek enlightenment, happiness, or love shall not find it. for life is a journey, not a destination

if you need to read this, then once again the world is drowned in hatred. hate is such a simple, easy message — “hate them, for they are the cause of your suffering”. the abusers want you to believe only that hate is easy, that love — love is impossible.

one sentence version: change is interesting, discuss. or “a positive version of jared diamond’s ‘collapse’, with why and how–not instead of how.”

  • filtering the world through a lens of change/binding — change being the non–exclusive flipside of binding —what “makes” people abuse others? why do we interact the way we do?
    • so binding here in the sense both of bond and of addiction. how it enables abusive and destructive behaviour, on the worse side, and creative and civilising behaviour, on the better side. how it forms and decays with cognitive growth and decline. mental and societal models of process plus sundry speculations on this factor in the flourishing, decline, and fall of civilisations.

at its heart this book is about how to reject abuse and oppression, about how to cherish and love.

I have a knack of change and momentum, of comprehending just what it is that is holding someone or something from their potential. In the world as I write, the most awful is human abuse and irresponsibility. Nobody is completely responsible, everyone is abusive in some way. But what I call abusers are far, far beyond this. How is it that abusers exist, perpetuate, abuse — and how do we do better?

the idea of reaching for our higher potential, what holds us back, and how to transcend it.

unusual in that want not only to do most actions unconsciously but also to periodically consciously review those unconscious habits

want to write a book for people like me, who can fit in anywhere and adapt to any situation but also carry their examined values with them.

we tend to want to believe about the world what we believe in ourselves. i believe all people can do extraordinary things, so i don't believe i am particularly extraordinary. a cynical person, on the other hand, sees the reflection of himself in the world.

  • unlike some people’s opinions of their own prowess, I believe most of what I am able to do is from chance circumstance that enabled the development of abilities that are inherent in all of us.
  • from the available evidence of my close genetic relatives I appear to have a partly genetic propensity to strong self–will. but here’s the odd–sounding thing. I also believe that almost everyone is strong–willed. it’s just that our strong wills are not always, or possibly even rarely, aligned with the best interests of the earth, humankind, or even our supposed individual values.

why is world the way it is, what can we do about it, and how do we go about doing it?

DIP design in process / design in progress

re–evaluate what we do as a process so that we move towards progress

makes us more “relentless” and more “ruthless” — literally we relent less and regret less what we do, which initially might seem odd, but if you think about it a bit, it starts to make sense.

for example, if we do what we need to do, instead of not doing it, then it is easier to keep it up and later we cannot spend time regretting it. or if we always tell the truth, instead of lying and using the energy to maintain a separate version of reality, we can put our energy towards making our world a better place.

this process means that incrementally, little by little, each action we take contributes towards progress and steadily, surely, we become better than we were, better than the abusers as they cannot do the same.

three inter–related parts — book, mirror, sword. learn what we need, re–evaluate what we do, do it.

it is like a sail — instead of having to rely on the wind coming from behind us, as the abusers do, we can use the winds that blow in other directions — by deflecting them with our sails we can gain energy to move in the direction we choose — we are no longer only buffeted by the winds of fate, we can turn their momentum towards progress.

we can still sometimes lose our way, the winds can still sometimes blow us off course, but by using this process, as only we can, we will be blown less off course and more rarely than abusive people are.

the real world is not like an engine which we can feed and direct at our whim, it is like the wind and the sun — fates blowing in many directions and steadily, surely moving us each day. when we use the energy of the wind and the sun, as they can be used, then we move in ways the abusers cannot even hope to.

for those who still take aeroplanes, here is a little story. one day you are about to go to the airport to catch a plane. somehow you know if you don't get that flight something terrible, awful, is going to happen. and for some reason, all the phones and internet are broken, you can't get through to anyone to warn them. but you're not old enough to drive and your parents won't let you cycle because it's too dangerous — and it's almost time for the flight and they don't even know how to get to the airport but they are sitting on their assets in the living room watching television. finally, through it is well past your departure time, you manage to chivvy them into the car, but they say they can't drop you there, it's too far and too much trouble. maybe the flight is delayed, maybe, just maybe catching a later flight will be ok and you can still avert the disaster. so now they drop you as far as they will go, you have to walk from miles away, with your luggage. so you abandon it and start running. your friend who can cycle helps you find the way. the kind staff at the airport radio ahead when you get there to ask them to hold the plane. but now you are in the clear tunnels leading to the plane but they twist and turn and have no exits except the ones labelled with numbers and none of them are yours and you run and run up the stairs and you can see the plane but not the way there. that is where we are right now.
