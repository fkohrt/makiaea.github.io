adaptive nature of diversity

we make the mistake of believing that only those who are similar to us can be our friends — a simplistic rendering of community and society

holistic asian vs analytic western demonstrates adaptive nature of having diversity — people born to one culture can learn from the other, and under some conditions one may be more adaptive than the other, while in other situations the adaptive advantage may be in the other direction.

uniqueness is valuable, as is conformity. actually everyone is unique, and conformity is an illusion, an idea for which there is a use. we need a kind of conformity in order to create and maintain a society. bullying is one example of the way the idea called conformity is enforced.

  • conformity eases the ability of abusive people to manipulate people in ways that suit the ends of the abuser.

complex issues are not necessarily difficult problems. they are difficult when our knowledge and experience is specialised but they are much easier when we have broad knowledge and experience. same reason for survival as for fast development. illusion again that we are specialist, everyone has many talents, whether we realise or not.

A generalist is valuable where generalists are valued, but also where they are not valued.

Because a generalist has diverse abilities, they are less often held back by the obstacle of missing vital links in information or ability, or may be more able to readily compensate for such missing links, or may be more able to switch to or work on alternative approaches or issues until conditions are more favourable.

The process of integrating and understand and acting is part of what life is, and the ability to adapt to and deal with obstacles becomes, as time accumulates, an incredibly powerful force — a super–power that each of us has within ourselves. This is a lesson we should learn from water and how it flows.
