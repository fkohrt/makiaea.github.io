    • cut hair
      • for feathering effect, lift a section of hair from the edge (e.g. fringe) you are cutting, lifting between the straight sides of two fingers just above the edge, with palm facing scalp. allow hair to slide between the fingers and aim to position the hair that is directly under the gap between the fingers so that it is now perpendicular to the scalp. cut a small section by placing the scissors alongside the fingers on the back side of the hand. the geometry of the lifted hair means that there will be a feathering effect. the same type of geometry occurs if you bunch all the hair to the top of the head to create a pony tail rising from the top of the head, and then cut it parallel to the scalp (“horizontal to the ground”) just above the nexus of the bunch
